RSA Encryption

This RSA Encryption application is an asynchronous web implementation of asymmetrical encryption, the concept of encrypting messages one way, using a depryption key kept secret to the encrypting client.

Fascinated by the world of CyberSecurity, I've been looking into how data is encrypted, and much to my surprise, even after the encryption, it is still very insecure! If two computers have to tell each other how to encrypt messages, then what is to stop a third party from listening to the network to get ahold of the encryption method so that they can decrypt it? This question led me to asymmetrical encryption.

Details

The first step to any good encryption algorithm is to choose our prime numbers. The reason we use prime numbers is because when we do operations with these numbers, it is incredibly difficult and time intensive to find large prime factors of our products. To generate a prime number, I basically just repeatedly generated random numbers, and then using the Miller-Rabin primality test, checked if those random numbers were prime or not. The Miller-Rabin primality test is a probabilistic test that fairly accurately tells when a number is prime. It does this be generating a number of tests that would give us evidence that it is prime or not. There are going to be some non prime numbers that slip through, however they should be far and few in between.
